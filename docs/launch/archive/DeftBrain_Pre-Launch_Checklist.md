5. **Launch activities** — Marketing and announcement planning.

Clearance picture is favorable. No direct or phonetic conflicts with `DEFTBRAIN` identified in the USPTO federal register. Crowded-field conditions in the `*BRAIN` AI-software space support registrability of the distinctive `DEFT` modifier.

### 1.1 Searches performed (USPTO tmsearch.uspto.gov)

- `CM:deftbrain* AND LD:true`

- `(CM:deftbrain OR CM:"deft brain" OR CM:deft*brain*) AND LD:false` (dead marks)

### 1.2 Key findings

**No direct or near-phonetic conflicts.** Across 147 results, zero marks contain `DEFT`. No `DAFT`, `DEPT`, `DEFB`, `DEPH`, or `DEFF` variants. The `DEFT` modifier is unclaimed in this space.

**Only 5 `D*BRAIN` marks exist**, all distinguishable:

- DECLOAKBRAIN — privacy/encryption software (Taiwan)
- DIGITAL BRAIN
- DISHBRAIN — lab tech (Germany)
- DEALBRAIN — deal management (DoneDeal AI)
- DOCBRAIN — document processing (Belgium)

**Crowded-field evidence.** 133 live `*BRAIN` marks are registered or pending in Class 042 with "artificial intelligence" in the goods/services description. Single owners hold large `*BRAIN` portfolios, supporting the argument that the `BRAIN` suffix functions as a weak formative rather than a source identifier:

- WPP Properties — 8 parallel marks (WPP BRAND BRAIN, WPP AUDIENCE BRAIN, AUDIENCE BRAIN, PERFORMANCE BRAIN, BRAND BRAIN, WPP PERFORMANCE BRAIN, WPP CHANNEL BRAIN, CHANNEL BRAIN)
- Kakao Corp. — 3 marks (BRAINLM, BRAINMM, BRAINIM)
- CBRAIN A/S — 3 CBRAIN variants
- Mango Technologies (ClickUp) — BRAINGPT, CLICKUP BRAIN, BRAIN MAX

**Filing velocity is increasing.** 133 `*BRAIN` AI marks filed over 2015–2026, with filings approximately doubling year-over-year since 2022 (2023: 19; 2024: 26; 2025: 36; 2026 YTD through April: 17). Argues for filing sooner rather than later.

**Goods/services language precedent.** Block 1 contains well-drafted G/S descriptions for AI-productivity software, including Mango Technologies' (ClickUp) filings for BRAINGPT / CLICKUP BRAIN / BRAIN MAX with carve-out language ("*none of the foregoing to constitute supply chain management software*"), which appears to have been added in response to an examiner citation and may serve as useful precedent language.

### 1.3 Scope limitations of this search

- Federal register only. No common-law (unregistered use) search was performed.
- No state registration search.
- No international search beyond what is recorded in USPTO data on foreign applicants.
- No domain / social handle search beyond the known `deftbrain.com` ownership.

## 2. Trademark Filing Plan (For Attorney Review)

### 2.1 Questions for counsel

1. Given the clearance picture above, any registrability concerns the applicant is missing?
2. Recommended filing classes. Applicant's working assumption is **IC 009** (downloadable software components, if any) and **IC 042** (SaaS), following the Mango Technologies / ClickUp pattern. Class 041 (educational content) may also be warranted given certain tools in the suite.
3. Recommended filing basis — 1(a) use-in-commerce (DeftBrain is live at deftbrain.com) vs. 1(b) intent-to-use. Applicant's first-use date is documented.
4. Standard-character mark vs. stylized/design mark. Both? Which first?
5. TEAS Plus vs. TEAS Standard for filing.
6. Any concern re: ClickUp / Mango Technologies as an active prosecutor in adjacent name-space? Risk of opposition?
7. Is a paid common-law search (Thomson CompuMark, Corsearch) warranted given the clean federal picture and active use?
8. Counsel's flat-fee quote for filing (per class) and typical office-action response fee.
9. If counsel handles filing, expected timeline to registration under normal USPTO pacing.

### 2.2 Materials the applicant can provide

- This document

- Screenshots of deftbrain.com showing the mark in use in connection with the offered AI tools (for 1(a) specimen)

### 2.3 Applicant's provisional filing preferences

- 1(a) filing basis
- Willing to hire counsel for the filing itself if flat fee is proportionate to the asset value
