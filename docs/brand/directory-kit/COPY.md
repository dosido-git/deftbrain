## Long description (1 paragraph)

> Personal AI problem solvers for moments too specific or personal for search
> engines. Most AI tools are generic chatbots trying to do everything.
> DeftBrain is the opposite: small, focused AI tools, each built for a
> specific, real problem — not "write me an email," but "how do I get my full
> security deposit back" or "is this lease going to screw me." Some tools help
> you understand something confusing: what your doctor actually said, what a
> contract actually commits you to, whether a product review is real. Others
> help you say or write something difficult: an apology that doesn't make
> things worse, a complaint that actually gets a response. A few solve
> uncommon problems nothing else covers: the word on the tip of your tongue,
> what to do during a long layover, how to get a fee waived. Every tool is
> free, no signup required. Paste your situation, get a specific, real answer
> — not the one your neighbor got.

## First-comment draft (Product Hunt — hold PH for last per Notes sheet)

> Maker here. DeftBrain started from a simple observation: most people don't
> have a prompting problem, they have a *problem* problem — a lease they don't
> understand, a deposit they want back, an apology they need to get right.
> Generic chatbots make you do the work of asking well. So we built 120+ tiny
> tools that each ask the right questions for exactly one situation and give
