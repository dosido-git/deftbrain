# DeftBrain — Voice & Positioning Vocabulary

A working reference for how DeftBrain describes itself: canonical lines, tagline
candidates, and the reasoning behind them. Nothing in this file changes the site
by itself — it exists so good lines stop living in lost notes. When a line ships
(masthead, About, directory listing), note where.

Written 2026-07-05, from a working session with The Operator.

---

## The positioning argument (the thing every good line expresses)

> **"Intelligence is abundant; application is precious."**
> — The Operator's formulation, 2026-07-05. The canonical statement of the
> thesis, and a candidate line in its own right.

Everyone has a chatbot in their pocket; what remains precious is
**application** — knowing what to ask, in the specific moment, about the
specific problem. DeftBrain's 128 tools are that application work, pre-done.
The tagline family that wins is the one that claims the *aim*, not the
*intelligence*.

Corollary insight — **first-time problems**: most DeftBrain moments (a deposit
fight, a diagnosis, a layover, an overwhelming project) happen to a given
person once or rarely. Nobody builds skill at them. That's why a pre-aimed
specialist beats accumulated experience, and why the catalog is 128 wide.

## Tagline candidates (unshipped)

**Leading contenders for the masthead slot:**
- **"Applied Intelligence"** — The Operator's. A-. Claims the scarce thing;
  echoes applied/pure science; re-expands the acronym (AI = Applied
  Intelligence — a free second line of copy everywhere). Caution: faint
  consultancy scent (Accenture ran a division by this name); fine as a tagline,
  crowded as a name.
- **"See a specialist."** — wittier, human, implies the catalog via a medical
  idiom everyone already understands.

**The AI re-expansion family:** Actual Intelligence · AI, aimed. · "The other
AI: applied intelligence." (About-page explainer regardless of masthead).

**The specialist family:** 128 specialists. No waiting room. · A specialist
for that.

**The first-time-problems family** (the most insightful vein):
- "Expertise for problems you'll only have once."
- "For problems you don't get practice at."
- "A second brain for first-time problems."

**The anti-blank-page family:** AI without the blank page. · The end of
"where do I start?" · Never start from scratch.

**The utility family:** Thinking, on tap. · Judgment, on tap. (bolder; mind
the Terms page).

**The name family:** Be deft about it. · Deftness, borrowed.

**The P-triad** (The Operator's: "Prompt, perceptive, precious."): prompt +
perceptive are a true pair — both double-mean on brand (fast/made-of-prompts/
prompts-you; notices-what's-hidden). Caution on self-applied "precious"
(pejorative shadow: fussy); it's protected in the thesis sentence, exposed in
a self-descriptive triad. Variants: "Prompt, perceptive, precise." ·
"Prompt, perceptive, practical." Strongest construction — the dictionary
entry, which makes the name itself do the work:

> **deft** *(adj.)* — prompt, perceptive, precise.

Refinement (The Operator, via the actual dictionary): the REAL definition
beats the invented triad, because of its last three words —

> **deft** *(adj.)* — showing cleverness and skill **in handling things**.

"Handling things" IS the product; played straight, it's a definition rather
than a claim, so the reader supplies the connection. About-page opener
version: "The dictionary defines deft as 'showing cleverness and skill in
handling things.' That's the whole ambition here." Synonym ladder for body
copy: apt, adept, adroit, skillful (the skill-words are hand-words — adroit
= "to the right"; suits a site about handling). Trivia landmine: deft and
daft share a root; dinner party yes, About page no.

**Evolution of the incumbent:** "Intelligence, already aimed."

## One-liners (descriptions, not taglines)

1. "The same AI everyone else has — better briefed, 128 times."
2. "128 conversations with an expert, each one starting at the second question."
3. "A general intelligence, pre-aimed at 128 specific problems."
4. "Each tool is the first twenty minutes of figuring out what to ask — already done."
5. "Expertise with a form in front of it." (the form is the point — it knows what to ask *you*)
6. "A toolbox where every tool already knows what job it's for."
7. "A vending machine for judgment calls."
8. "Not another place to chat with an AI — 128 reasons you won't have to."
9. "The difference between a blank page and a form that knows your situation."
10. "Applied AI, in the literal sense: someone already applied it."

Picks: #1 for technical readers, #5 for the shortest true thing, #10 for the
one that makes someone pause.

## Ten words

**Specific · Applied · Deft · Practical · Immediate · Anonymous · Focused ·
Broad · Plainspoken · Humane**

Compressed to three: **specific, deft, humane** — what it does, how it does
it, why it exists.

Sleeper word: **prompt** — triple-means on brand (fast; built from prompts;
each tool prompts *you* with the right questions). "DeftBrain is prompt." is a
tagline masquerading as a sentence. Use as a running motif in body copy.

Weak words to avoid leaning on: assist, aid (true but generic).

## The verb taxonomy (what the 128 tools actually do)

The Operator's word list, mapped — nearly every tool does exactly one of these:

| Verb | The job | Example tools |
|---|---|---|
| **Reveal** | what's hidden in this? | LeaseTrapDetector, FakeReviewDetective, MarkupDetective, MeetingBSDetector |
| **Analyze** | what's really going on? | NameAudit, SocialEnergyAudit, CrashPredictor |
| **Advise** | what should I do? | BuyWise, LaundroMat, SkillGapMap |
| **Advocate** | help me fight this | RentersDepositSaver, ComplaintEscalationWriter, MoneyDiplomat |
| **Resolve** | settle it | TheFinalWord, ApologyCalibrator |
| **Assemble** | build me the thing | TaskAvalancheBreaker, BragSheetBuilder |
| **Guide** | show me the way | the guides, ToolFinder |

**Advocate** is the underrated one — it names the emotional job: some tools
don't inform you, they take your side.

Constructions from the taxonomy:
- **"Analyze. Advise. Advocate."** — an escalation ladder (understand →
  recommend → fight for you); the punchy-slot pick.
- "Aid, applied."
- Directory copy: "128 tools that analyze, advise, advocate, reveal, resolve,
  and assemble — promptly."

## Venue guidance

- **Masthead / brand furniture** — two-to-three word register: Applied
  Intelligence · See a specialist. · (incumbent: Intelligence on Demand)
- **Directories / Product Hunt** — name the moment: the "oddly specific
  moments" family; rotate per the directory kit.
- **About page / interviews** — the arguments: first-time problems; the other
  AI; the blank page.
- **Body copy anywhere** — the *prompt* motif; the verb taxonomy for section
  structure.
