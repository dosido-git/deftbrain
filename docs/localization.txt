Beyond language translation, a complete localization suite covers:

**Formatting**
- Numbers (`1,234.56` vs `1.234,56`)
- Currency (symbol position, decimal places, local currency vs display currency)
- Dates (`MM/DD/YYYY` vs `DD/MM/YYYY` vs `YYYY-MM-DD`)
- Time (12hr vs 24hr, timezone display)
- Percentages and units of measure (imperial vs metric)

**Text & Typography**
- Plural rules (many languages have 3–6 plural forms, not just singular/plural)
- Gender agreement (Spanish, French, German adjective agreement)
- Text directionality — RTL layout for Arabic, Hebrew, Farsi (flips entire UI)
- Font support for non-Latin scripts (Cyrillic, Arabic, CJK, Devanagari)
- Text expansion — German and Finnish can run 30–40% longer than English

**Regional Conventions**
- Address formats (field order varies dramatically by country)
- Phone number formats (country codes, spacing, length)
- Name order (given/family reversal in East Asian cultures)
- Legal disclaimers (jurisdiction-specific)

**Content**
- Images and icons that may carry cultural meaning (thumbs up is offensive in some regions)
- Color associations (white = mourning in some cultures; red = luck vs danger)
- Examples and reference content (tool examples should feel locally relevant)

**Technical**
- Character encoding (already handled by UTF-8, but worth confirming throughout)
- Sorting/collation (alphabetical order differs by locale)
- Input method support (IME for CJK)

**For DeftBrain specifically**, the highest-leverage gaps after language are likely: **plural rules**, **RTL layout**, and **number/date formatting** — in that priority order, based on where tools are most likely to render garbled output for non-English users.
