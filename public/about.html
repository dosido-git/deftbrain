About DeftBrain

The idea

A general chatbot is a blank page. When something specific is happening — your lease has a clause that smells wrong, your doctor said four words you didn't understand, a project is so overwhelming you can't start it — a blank page is the last thing you need.

DeftBrain is 128 tools that each do one job. Each tool asks only for what it needs, sends it to an AI with instructions refined for that exact situation, and gives you back something structured you can act on: the trap in the clause, the plain-English diagnosis, the two-minute first task. No prompt engineering. No account. No blank page.

Why it's free

The honest answer: DeftBrain is in its validation stage. The goal right now is to find out which of these tools genuinely help people — not to monetize them. There is no catch waiting behind a signup wall, because there is no signup. If some tools prove genuinely useful, some version of a business may follow; the free tools you use today are how we find out which ones deserve to exist.

How it works

Every tool runs on Claude, Anthropic's AI model, with instructions we've written and tested per tool. What you type is sent to our server, passed to Anthropic to generate your result, and returned to you. We don't store your input or your results — not in a database, not in logs. The details, including exactly which third parties are involved, are in the privacy policy, which we'd genuinely encourage you to read because for once it's short and contains good news.

What these tools are not

Several tools deal with serious subjects — leases, medical visits, mental health, personal safety, money. They are preparation tools, not professionals. LeaseTrapDetector helps you walk into a lawyer's office with better questions; it is not a lawyer. DoctorVisitTranslator helps you understand what you heard; it is not a doctor. AI models can be wrong, confidently. Where a tool touches a decision that matters, treat its output as a well-organized starting point and verify what's load-bearing.

Who makes this

DeftBrain is built and run by one person from Massachusetts, USA — known here, in keeping with the privacy policy's own phrasing, as "O", The Operator. One human, no parent company, no investors, no advertisers. Every tool was designed, tested, and — when it broke — fixed by the same Operator currently rereading this sentence with mild embarrassment.

The product is meant to speak for itself; The Operator prefers it that way.

The guides

The guides are written with AI assistance and edited by hand, and each one exists to answer a question people actually ask on the way to one of the tools. When a guide is thin, we consolidate it rather than let it pad the site. Dates on each guide are real.
